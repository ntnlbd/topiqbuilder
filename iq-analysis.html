The abstract reasoning test assesses your logical, analytical and conceptual skills. It evaluates people’s ability to perceive patterns among complex ideas and elements and relate them to each other. As a measure, it is independent of educational attainments and it can reveal people’s intellectual potential. Your score on this test was relatively low compared to the reference group. Your results indicate that you experienced a significant difficulty to use pure logic without calling upon other knowledge and correctly deduce the logical consequences of complex abstract patterns and relationships. This means that although you usually work out problems for yourself, you often cannot deal with ideas that are not fully explained or precise. You often need to spend a lot of effort and time in order to get along with courses or occupations that require the ability to see relationships among objects in terms of their size, shape, position and quantities or put abstract ideas in a conceptual context.

Ways to improve your performance in abstract reasoning tests and your abstract skills in general could include:

  • Spend some time to focus on the question and make sure you understand what you are being asked to do
  • Identify patterns that are useful to complete other similar questions
  • Break the question into smaller, more manageable pieces to help you better understand what you need to do
  • Practice by solving logical games and puzzles
  • Spend some time examining information that is presented in abstract forms

You will be able to benefit from training materials once you are a member of the Intelluni Intelligence Club. You will have the chance to practice with relationships, connections and sequences which should help you improve both your accuracy and speed not only in this kind of tests but also in your life in general.
