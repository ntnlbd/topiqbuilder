    Question and answer

    What does IQ mean and what does it stand for?

    IQ stands for “Intelligence Quotient” and is a score derived from a test designed to assess human intelligence. The abbreviation "IQ" was coined by psychologist William Stern from the German term “Intelligenz quotient”

    Can my IQ score be improved or is it set for life?

    Most psychologists agree that it can and does improve through various factors including age, education and specific training programs. An IQ test measures your innate ability to reason in different ways and your brain can be trained and improve over time.

    Does an IQ test completely sums up someone’s intelligence?

    No it doesn’t. Experts agree that an IQ test ignores the emotional aspect of an individual which is a crucial aspect of someone’s overall intelligence considering we live in society. Emotional Intelligence tests such as EQ tests were consequently introduced.

    What does EQ mean and what does it stand for?

    EQ stands for “Emotional Quotient” and measures Emotional Intelligence which is the ability to monitor his/her own and other people's emotions, to differentiate between various emotions and label them appropriately and to use emotional information to guide thinking and behaviour.

    Am I dumb if my IQ score is below the average score?

    No you are not. As mentioned, there are different types of intelligence and you cannot be summarized by an IQ score. Emotional intelligence is another important factor to who you are. Also, please remember that your brain can be trained to become more efficient.
